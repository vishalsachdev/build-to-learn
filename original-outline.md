## Theory of Mind for AI

Your capacity to model another agent's beliefs, goals, and perspective.

When you get a bad response, ask:
- "What false assumption is the AI making?"
- "What context am I taking for granted?"

**Your job is to be the bridge:** Intent <-----> AI's understanding

Theory of Mind is your capacity to model what another agent knows, believes, and needs. When you get a bad AI response, don't just rephrase. Ask: What false assumption is the AI making? What context am I taking for granted that it doesn't have? YOUR JOB IS TO BE THE BRIDGE between your intent and the AI's understanding.

---

> "If I ask AI to 'make a quiz for my students,' it doesn't know their reading level, what we just covered, whether it's formative or summative, or that half my class are ELL students. My job is to be the bridge ‚Äî to translate my tacit knowledge into explicit context."

---

## Theory of Mind ‚Üí Context Engineering

**PROMPT ENGINEERING to CONTEXT ENGINEERING**

- Prompt engineering sounds like wordsmithing ‚Äî finding the magic phrase, the clever trick
- Context engineering is about what information you include, not how cleverly you phrase it
- You don't need to learn prompting. You need to make your tacit knowledge explicit

So if the skill is recognizing what context the AI is missing, the next question is: how do you actually provide that context? This is where we shift from 'prompt engineering' to 'context engineering.

---

## The Three Stances

- **Don't know the domain** ‚Üí AI gives garbage mixed with gold, can't tell which
- **Know domain, resist adapting** ‚Üí Only get time savings on rote tasks
- **Know domain + collaborate creatively** ‚Üí DRAMATIC amplification

Ben Goertzel - an AI researcher - describes three stances people take. Stance 1: You don't know the domain, so AI outputs garbage mixed with gold and you can't tell which is which. Stance 2: You know your domain but resist adapting - you only get time savings on rote tasks. Stance 3: You know your domain AND collaborate creatively - that's where dramatic amplification happens. You are domain experts in learning. Today we move you to Stance 3.

---

## Architect, Not Coder

You describe what you/students need.  
The AI builds it.  
Your expertise is pedagogy, not HTML/JavaScript.

Mental model 3: You're the architect, not the coder. You describe what students need. The AI builds it. Your expertise is pedagogy, not JavaScript. Never let 'I can't code' stop you.

---

## Iterate, Don't Perfect

Get prompt perfect first time  
‚Üí  

Describe what's wrong

Your first prompt won't be right. That's expected.

Mental model 4: Iterate, don't perfect. Your first prompt won't be right - that's expected and fine. Don't try to craft the perfect prompt. Instead, describe what's wrong with what you got. This is exactly how professionals work with AI.
