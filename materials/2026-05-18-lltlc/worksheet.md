# **ğŸ§  Mental Models**

## **1\. Theory of Mind for AI**

* The skill is social, not technical  
* Ask: "What context is the AI missing? What assumption is it making?"  
* Your job: be the bridge between your intent and AI's understanding

## **2\. Context Engineering \> Prompt Engineering**

* It's not about magic phrases â€” it's about what information you include  
* Make tacit knowledge explicit: course level/modality, purpose, constraints, what didn't work before

## **3\. The Three Stances**

* **Stance 1:** Don't know domain â†’ can't tell garbage from gold  
* **Stance 2:** Know domain, resist adapting â†’ only rote task savings  
* **Stance 3:** Know domain \+ collaborate creatively â†’ **dramatic amplification**

## **4\. Architect, Not Coder**

You describe what you or your students need; AI builds it. Your expertise is in describing your intent well, not JavaScript or python. 

## **5\. Iterate, Don't Perfect**

First prompt/output won't be right â€” that's expected. Describe what's wrong with what you got.

# **ğŸ”¨ Build Session 2: Prompts That Work**

### **Starting your build with** [aistudio.google.com](https://aistudio.google.com)

**Steps:**
1. Go to [aistudio.google.com](https://aistudio.google.com) (use personal Gmail if work account is blocked)
2. Click **"Build"** in the top menu
3. Export your Excalidraw sketch as PNG, or describe what you want
4. Paste the image or type your description
5. Iterate with follow-up messages!

**Starter prompt template:**

*"Build a \[tool type\] for \[course level / modality\] students that \[what it does\]. Include \[specific features\] and consider accessibility."*

### **Iteration prompts:**

* "Make the button bigger and more visible"  
* "Add a score counter in the top right"  
* "Change the color scheme to \[X\]"  
* "Make it work better on mobile/tablet"  
* "Add a reset button"  
* "The \[X\] isn't working â€” it should \[expected behavior\]"

### **When stuck:**

* Describe what you *see* vs. what you *expected*  
* Add context: "This is for a first-year course with varied preparation and accessibility needs"  
* Ask yourself: "What context is the AI missing?"

# **ğŸ“ Teaching Students to Build**

Once you've built something yourself, consider having your students do the same!

### Why students should build:
- **Agency** â€” When students build their own study tools, they own their learning
- **Iteration** â€” "Fix what's wrong" beats "get it right the first time"
- **Curiosity** â€” "What else could I build?" is the question we want them asking

### Student-friendly project ideas:
- Flashcards for their own vocabulary/concepts
- A quiz to test themselves or classmates
- A study guide or review tool
- A timer for their own study sessions
- A "teach-back" tool to explain a concept

### Tips for course implementation:
1. **Start simple** â€” Pick ONE class session or module where students build instead of consume
2. **Model first** â€” Show them your own building process (including mistakes!)
3. **Embrace iteration** â€” Let them revise and improve
4. **Share outcomes** â€” Have students demo their tools to each other

*The same mental models apply: Theory of Mind, Context Engineering, Architect not Coder, Iterate don't Perfect*
