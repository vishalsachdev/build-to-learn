## Slide 5: Why Building Works (3 Principles)

**Agency**
When you build something real, you own it. Ownership drives engagement.

**Iteration**
Learning happens through: attempt ‚Üí feedback ‚Üí refinement
(Not "get it right the first time")

**Curiosity**
Building something real sparks questions that worksheets never will.
Students ask "What else could I build?" instead of "When is this due?"

## Slide 6: Building Deepens Understanding (NEW - Epistemology)

## Slide 7-11: Mental Models (Reordered by Importance to Ad)

### Slide 7: Theory of Mind for AI

"The skill that makes you great at AI isn't technical. It's social."
‚Äî Riedl & Weidmann, "Quantifying Human-AI Synergy" (2025)

Your capacity to model another agent's beliefs, goals, and perspective.

When you get a bad response, ask:
- "What false assumption is the AI making?"
- "What context am I taking for granted?"

Your job: **Be the bridge** between intent ‚Üî AI's understanding

**Example**: Don't say "make a quiz." Say "make a 5-question formative quiz for 4th graders who just learned fractions, include one trick question, and show the answer after they submit."

---

### Slide 8: Context Engineering > Prompt Engineering

Prompt engineering = wordsmithing (magic phrases, tricks)

**Context engineering = making tacit knowledge explicit**

You don't need to learn prompting tricks.
You need to translate: 
- Course level / modality
- What you covered
- What didn't work before
- Constraints and edge cases
- Who your audience is

**This skill transfers everywhere.** Not dependent on Claude or ChatGPT.

### Slide 9: Problem First, Tool Second (NEW - From Teaching Philosophy)

**The difference between a tool students use and a tool students care about:**

| Tool as Template | Tool as Solution to Your Pain |
|---|---|
| "Make a flashcard app" | "I spend 20 min/week organizing a concept glossary for students with varied preparation" |
| Generic | Specific |
| One iteration | 10 iterations |
| Forgotten after class | Used for months |

**Your job today**: Identify YOUR pain point.
Not what's on this list. What's YOURS?

"What would make my teaching life easier?"
"What do my students struggle with?"
"What takes too long?"

Then build THAT.

### Slide 13: Iterate, Don't Perfect

Your first output won't be right.
That's expected and good.

Don't try to craft the perfect prompt upfront.

Version 1 is a starting point, not a failure.

## Slide 17b: Screenshot + Remix (Fastest Path)

For people who struggle to ideate from scratch:

1. **Screenshot** any website, PDF, app, or tool you like
2. **Paste** into Excalidraw (or upload directly)
3. **Annotate** with text: "Change this to X", "Add Y here"
4. **Upload** to AI Studio ‚Äî it creates your customized version

**Why this works:**
- No blank page problem ‚Äî you have a starting point
- Shows the AI exactly what you want
- Your annotations = your customizations
- Output: single HTML+JS page you can publish via Netlify Drop
